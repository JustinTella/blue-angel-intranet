Prescription step-ups by situation
The basic kit is the foundation. These are layered on top based on destination risk profile.
SituationStep-up prescriptionWhy basic kit falls short
Malaria-endemicMalarone or Doxy or MefloquineNo prophylaxis in basic kit
High altitudeDiamox + DexamethasoneNo altitude coverage
SE Asia / South AsiaAzithromycin upgrade + RifaximinCipro resistance-compromised — essentially useless for TD here
Jungle / remoteCoartem + TinidazoleNo breakthrough malaria Rx or protozoa coverage

Scenario 01
Malaria-endemic
Sub-Saharan Africa · SE Asia rural · South Asia · Central America rural · Amazon · Papua New Guinea
→
Malarone (atovaquone-proguanil)Daily. Start 1–2 days before, stop 7 days after. First-line default per CDC Yellow Book.
→
Doxycycline 100mgDaily. Budget alternative. Stop 4 weeks after. Also covers rickettsial and leptospirosis.
→
Mefloquine 250mgWeekly. For 4+ week trips. Neuropsychiatric screen required.
Scenario 02
High altitude
Cusco · La Paz · Kilimanjaro · Mt Kenya · Ethiopian highlands · Himalayan treks · Tibet · Ladakh
→
Diamox (acetazolamide) 125mgTwice daily. Start day before ascent. Reduces AMS risk ~48% per CDC Yellow Book.
→
Dexamethasone 4mgEmergency backup only — not prophylaxis. For severe AMS/HACE if descent not possible.
Scenario 03
SE Asia / South Asia
India · Nepal · Bangladesh · Thailand · Cambodia · Vietnam · Laos · Myanmar · Indonesia · Pakistan
→
Azithromycin 500mg upgradeCipro is unreliable here — high fluoroquinolone resistance. Azithromycin becomes primary TD antibiotic.
→
RifaximinNon-absorbed. Minimal side effects. Add-on for prophylaxis in high street food exposure situations.
Scenario 04
Jungle / remote
Congo Basin · Amazon · Rural Papua New Guinea · Remote SE Asia jungle · Sub-Saharan bush
→
Coartem (artemether-lumefantrine)Standby breakthrough malaria treatment when local care unavailable. Distinct from prophylaxis.
→
Doxycycline 100mgLeptospirosis prophylaxis at 200mg weekly in flood/jungle environments.
→
Tinidazole or FlagylGiardia and protozoa prevalent in jungle water. Not covered by basic kit.
Scenario 05
Extended trip (6+ weeks)
Any malaria-endemic region · Long-term expat travel
→
MefloquineDisplaces Malarone/Doxy for long-haul. Weekly dosing makes 3+ month adherence realistic.
→
FluconazoleMonths of Doxy significantly raises yeast infection risk. Single-dose rescue.
Scenario 06
Freshwater exposure
Lake Malawi · Lake Tanganyika · Lake Victoria · Nile · Mekong · Amazon tributaries · Sub-Saharan rivers
→
Praziquantel (post-exposure)Not pre-travel — held for 6–8 weeks after freshwater exposure. Nearly all sub-Saharan freshwater is endemic.
Destination Protocols
Add-ons on top of the basic kit. Sourced from CDC Yellow Book 2026 and current CDC Travel Health Notices at time of query.
Zimbabwe
1 week · Sub-Saharan Africa
High risk▾
Active alerts: Malaria outbreak — 111,998 cases and 310 deaths as of June 2025 (up from 29,031 in 2024). East African sleeping sickness safari alert December 2024. Cholera active transmission. Falsified rabies vaccine circulating in country. Global measles alert May 2025.
Additional items
Malaria rapid test — fever within 1 year of visit requires immediate testing
Permethrin spray + treated bed net — treat all clothing before departure
Water purification tablets — tap water unsafe outside Harare
Medical evacuation insurance — strongly recommended given limited rural infrastructure
Advanced prescriptions
Malarone, 9 tabs — start 1–2 days before, daily, stop 7 days after. First choice for 1-week trip. ~$43–$70
Doxycycline 100mg — tick-borne illness treatment and leptospirosis. ~$11–$20
Flagyl (metronidazole) — Giardia and protozoa risk throughout the country. ~$10–$18
Vaccine program
8+ weeks before: Hepatitis A dose 1 · Hepatitis B dose 1 · Rabies pre-exposure dose 1 — strongly recommended due to counterfeit vaccine alert in country
4–6 weeks before: Hep B dose 2 · Rabies dose 2 · Typhoid injectable · Meningococcal MenACWY · Cholera (Vaxchora)
2–4 weeks before: Rabies dose 3 · Hep B dose 3 · Confirm MMR · Confirm Tdap · Polio booster
1–2 weeks before: Start Malarone · Annual flu shot · Yellow fever if routing through endemic country
Not needed: Japanese Encephalitis · TBE · Altitude medications (unless Eastern Highlands trek above 2,500m)
Jamaica
1 week · Caribbean
Moderate risk▾
Active alerts: Dengue endemic — 452 cases and 2 deaths in 2025. Leptospirosis outbreak December 2025 linked to freshwater exposure. Chikungunya and Zika both endemic. Global measles alert May 2025. No malaria — eliminated 2012.
Additional items
Dengue rapid test — fever with headache or rash within 2 weeks of return requires immediate testing
Extra waterproof bandages — all open wounds must be covered before any freshwater exposure (leptospirosis risk)
Condoms — HIV prevalence 1.3% general population, 30% MSM; high STI rates throughout
Advanced prescriptions
Doxycycline 100mg — leptospirosis prophylaxis at 200mg weekly per CDC Yellow Book for freshwater exposure
Tamiflu (oseltamivir) — if traveling October–May; flu peaks December–March in Jamaica
Vaccine program
4–6 weeks before: Hepatitis A dose 1 · Hepatitis B dose 1
2–4 weeks before: Hep B dose 2 · Confirm MMR · Confirm Tdap · Polio booster
1–2 weeks before: Hep B dose 3 · Annual flu shot
Not needed: Malaria prophylaxis · Cholera · Rabies (unless animal contact) · JE · TBE · Meningococcal · Yellow fever (unless routing through endemic country)
Botswana
8 days · Southern Africa
High risk▾
Active alerts: Malaria outbreak March 2025 — started in Okavango Delta, spread to non-endemic areas including Gaborone. Schistosomiasis in all freshwater. African tick-bite fever elevated in bush/safari areas. Polio cases confirmed. Global measles alert May 2025.
Additional items
Malaria rapid test — fever within 1 year of northern Botswana visit requires immediate testing
Permethrin spray + treated bed net — essential for Chobe and Okavango overnight stays
Dedicated tick removal tool — African tick-bite fever is common in game areas
Water purification tablets — rural and bush camp water not reliably safe
Medical evacuation insurance — Chobe and Okavango are remote with very limited hospital capacity
Advanced prescriptions
Malarone, 10 tabs — first choice for 8-day trip. Start 1–2 days before entering northern Botswana. ~$43–$70
Doxycycline 100mg — malaria alternative and tick-bite fever treatment. ~$11–$20
Flagyl (metronidazole) — protozoa risk outside hotels and lodges. ~$10–$18
Vaccine program
8+ weeks before: Hepatitis A dose 1 · Hepatitis B dose 1 · Rabies pre-exposure dose 1 — recommended for safari and bush travel
4–6 weeks before: Hep B dose 2 · Rabies dose 2 · Typhoid injectable · Meningococcal MenACWY
2–4 weeks before: Rabies dose 3 · Hep B dose 3 · Confirm MMR · Confirm Tdap · Polio booster
1–2 weeks before: Start Malarone · Annual flu shot · Yellow fever if routing through endemic country
Not needed: Japanese Encephalitis · TBE · Cholera · Dengue vaccine · Altitude medications
